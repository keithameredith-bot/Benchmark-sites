Oil Change Questions, Answered

Straight answers — the same ones we'd give you at the counter.

How much is an oil change in Ocala?

A full synthetic oil change is $79.95 and a diesel oil change is $129.95, both using premium Castrol oil. Every oil change includes a new filter, topped-off fluids, and a full inspection of your tires, brakes, belts, and fluids. Veterans, active military, and first responders save 20%.

How often should I change my oil?

It depends on your vehicle and the oil. As a general guide: conventional oil every 3,000-5,000 miles, synthetic blend around 5,000-7,500, and full synthetic often 7,500-10,000 — but always follow your owner's manual. Florida heat and stop-and-go driving can shorten those intervals, and we'll give you an honest recommendation for how you actually drive.

Synthetic or conventional - which do I need?

Modern engines, turbos, and newer vehicles usually call for full synthetic, and it holds up best in Florida heat. Older engines and tighter budgets can do fine on a blend or conventional. We'll check what your manufacturer specifies and never push you to a pricier oil your car doesn't need.

How long does an oil change take?

Most oil changes are done in about 30 minutes, and you're welcome to wait. Walk-ins are fine; call ahead if you'd like to lock in a time.

What's included in an oil change?

A new oil filter, fresh oil to your manufacturer's spec, topped-off fluids, a courtesy multi-point inspection, and responsible disposal of your old oil and filter. If we spot anything, we tell you straight - what needs attention and what can wait.

Do you offer a discount for veterans or first responders?

Yes. Veterans, active military, and first responders get 20% off every service, including oil changes - just let us know when you check in. Thank you for your service.

Do you service my make and model?

Almost certainly. We service all makes and models, foreign and domestic, from daily drivers to trucks. As a full auto-repair shop we can also handle anything the inspection turns up.
