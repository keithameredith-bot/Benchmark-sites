What a CV Axle Does

Your CV (constant velocity) axles carry power from the transmission to the wheels while flexing through every turn and bump. Each axle ends in greased joints sealed by rubber boots — and when a boot cracks, the grease slings out, road grit gets in, and the joint starts grinding itself to death. That's the clicking you hear on turns.

The Signs, In Order of Urgency

Clicking on Turns

The classic symptom — rhythmic clicking or popping when turning, especially under acceleration. That's a worn outer CV joint.

Vibration Under Power

Shudder or vibration that shows up when you accelerate and fades when you coast often points to an inner joint going bad.

Grease on the Wheel

Dark grease slung on the inside of a wheel or around the boot means the boot's torn. Caught early, sometimes the boot is the whole fix.

What Does CV Axle Replacement Cost?

It varies by vehicle — front-wheel drives, all-wheel drives, and 4x4s all package their axles differently, and parts prices follow. Two honest things we can promise: we diagnose first, because clicking and vibration aren't always the axle (wheel bearings and motor mounts fake these symptoms all the time), and you'll get the out-the-door price before we touch a wrench. Caught early enough, a torn boot can sometimes be fixed without replacing the whole axle — and if that's the case, that's what we'll tell you.

CV Axle Questions, Answered

Straight answers — the same ones we'd give you at the counter.

Why does my car click when I turn?

Rhythmic clicking on turns — especially while accelerating — is the textbook sign of a worn outer CV joint. It won't fix itself, and it gets more expensive the longer it grinds. Bring it by and we'll confirm whether it's the axle or something imitating it.

How much does CV axle replacement cost?

It depends on your vehicle and which axle — every drivetrain packages them differently. What's consistent: we diagnose first, quote you the full out-the-door price before any work, and tell you if a cheaper fix (like a boot, caught early) is on the table.

Can I keep driving with a clicking CV axle?

For a little while, probably — but it only gets worse, and a joint that fails completely can leave you with no drive power. Sooner is genuinely cheaper on this one.

Is it always the axle?

No — wheel bearings and motor mounts fake CV symptoms all the time. That's why we diagnose instead of guessing, so you're not paying for an axle when a bearing was the problem.
